📁 Case Study 1: Job Decoder & Resume Maker Funnel
1. The Problem
Most companies write job descriptions like they are trying to hire a PhD rocket scientist, when the reality is they just need a carpenter who knows how to use a hammer. HR departments load listings with high-level corporate jargon and fake complexity. This noise intimidates brilliant, highly-skilled candidates who actually have the practical knowledge to do the work, causing them to filter themselves out before they even apply because they don't speak "corporate BS."

2. What I Did & Decided
I built a two-part SaaS funnel: a free Resume Maker that leads directly into a premium utility called the Job Decoder. Instead of relying on brittle, keyword-matching Python rules that break whenever a company invents a new buzzword, I engineered the app to use LLMs to translate the text. It strips away the bloated filler, exposes the actual day-to-day tasks hidden underneath, and critiques the user's resume and cover letter to align precisely with the real work. I made a deliberate product choice to focus on making the translation visual, so users can instantly see exactly where they fit in the company’s layout.

3. What Came of It
It completely changes the dynamic of the job hunt. Instead of walking in as a nervous "prospect" begging for a chance, the user walks in ready to be an "employee on day one." By understanding exactly what is expected of them without the jargon barrier, applicants gain massive confidence. They can have an honest, direct conversation with recruiters, de-escalating their own interview anxiety because they know exactly what the company needs and how to speak to it.

📁 Case Study 2: One Push Alert
1. The Problem
Watching immigrant families and neighbors live in constant, silent anxiety over sudden ICE detentions and unexpected law enforcement encounters is horrifying. When a crisis happens, panic sets in, making it incredibly difficult to coordinate help. Standard group chats are too slow, and there was no fast, reliable way for someone to instantly sound the alarm to the right people before a high-stakes situation escalated out of control.

2. What I Did & Decided
I built One Push Alert, a single-button mobile safety application. I made a key strategic product decision: rather than building a loud, public-facing warning system that would draw political fire or put users in deeper conflict with authorities, I designed the app to route structured alerts privately. With one tap, the app sends a direct, hard record of the situation and coordinates to a pre-configured circle of safety—specifically family members, parents, and legal counsel.

3. What Came of It
This tool acts as a psychological anchor. In a tense confrontation, panic is the enemy; it makes it impossible to think clearly, which can escalate a routine stop into a dangerous situation. Having this single-button lifeline gives users the immediate peace of mind that they are not alone and that an immutable record is already safe in their lawyer's hands. That quiet confidence allows them to stay calm, think rationally, and safely de-escalate the interaction.
